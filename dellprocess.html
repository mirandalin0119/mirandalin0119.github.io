Dell's Product Webpage Redesign

Redesign Process

When I visited Dell's XPS 13 laptop's webpage, the first thing I noticed was that the page was very lengthy and presented a lot of information at once. Thus, my main goal in the redesign was to make the webpage simpler so that it serves as an introductory page to attract users to the XPS 13 model in general, and not a page that overwhelms users with various configuration details at once.

Note: This is purely a personal project, and not affiliated with Dell. Photos and information regarding the XPS 13 in courtesy of their official XPS 13 page, which can be accessed here.

Navigation Bar: Because my intention wasn't to redesign Dell's whole site, I kept their blue navigation bar the same to provide consistency between their different pages.

Introduction: In the brief introduction to the XPS 13 right after the navigation bar, the original page has a few lines of text on the left, and a picture/video of the laptop on the right. Though simple already, I decided to make this introductory section more visual by placing an image carousel in the center of the page with the text "XPS 13" on top.



Features: Right after the image carousel, I listed out four features of the XPS 13. Each feature is represented with an icon and a brief text. Below the four features, I have a "See All Features" button that would take the user to another page (or an overlay) where they'd be able to obtain more details. This is one of the biggest difference between my design and the original, where in the original, there is a designated "Features" section. Though informational, I believe that the original "Features" section contains more details than necessary on a main XPS 13 page, making the overall page too overwhelming. The left side of the image below is only one part of a lengthier "Features" section on Dell's page.



Configurations: Dell has 4 different filters located at the top of this section, including the laptop's hard drive, memory, operating system, and processor. In my design, I moved these filters to the left hand side instead. Additionally, instead of a dropdown menu, I changed the filter options to radio buttons. I believe that because there isn't a lot of options for each filter, radio buttons simplify the filtering process. I also decided to take out the Operating System filter, because there is only one option present (Windows 10).



I also simplified the configuration information displayed. In their design, Dell first includes summarized information regarding each configuration, with green "View Details" buttons at the end. Then, very detailed configuration information is listed, followed by another set of the green "View Details" buttons. Some of the information listed here is redundant; some details included here have already been included in the summarized section above. Though the design makes it easy for users to compare the different configurations at a detailed level, it also makes the webpage very clustered. Not to mention that Dell already has a comparison tool, as displayed by the "Add to compare" selection boxes next to each configuration, that can help users make comparisons.

In my redesign, I simplified this section by including only 4 pieces of information for each configuration. These are the same information that Dell listed in its summarized section. Following this, I also have "View Details" buttons, but I changed the color to blue to match the overall color scheme. To compare different configurations in detail, I kept Dell's comparison tool, but moved the "Add to Compare" selection boxes to below the "View Details" buttons. When users choose to compare the different configurations, then they will be displayed additional details like what Dell currently has. Otherwise, I eliminated the whole detailed configurations section from the web page.



Accessories: Dell split its accessories for XPS 13 into two sections, one being mobile essentials, and the other being work essentials. Each accessory has a picture along with a brief description. I decided to simplify this section by combining the two sections into just one "Essential Accessories" section. In this section, I display the accessories in two rows, and eliminated the descriptions for each accessory, keeping only the picture. To obtain more information regarding each section, the user can click on the accessory.



Support, Awards: In my redesign, I kept Dell's support section that provides brief descriptions of the three different support services. However, I decided to eliminate the Awards section.



Reviews: Dell presents this section with bar graph visualizations that provides an overall rating snapshot, followed by specific feedback from users. I simplified this section by keeping only the bar graph visualizations, and eliminated the individual feedbacks from the page. However, users can still see these individual reviews by clicking the "See all Reviews" button located at the bottom right corner of the section.



Questions, Drivers, Manuals & Support, Suggested Products: I decided to eliminate all 3 of these sections from the main page. I believe that these are important information that users may need, however, I believe that they don't all need to be displayed on the main XPS 13 page. Perhaps there can be links that direct users to these sections, but having such an abundance of information on the page makes the webpage very overwhelming.
